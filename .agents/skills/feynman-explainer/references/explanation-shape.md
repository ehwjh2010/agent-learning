# Feynman Explainer Shape

Default answer frame:

1. What it is
2. How it works
3. A concrete example or analogy
4. What people often get wrong
5. One-sentence summary

Quality bar:

- A beginner can follow it
- The explanation does not depend on undefined jargon
- The example reveals the mechanism instead of just decorating the answer
- The summary still makes sense when read alone

Avoid:

- Starting with a textbook definition and stopping there
- Using a chain of terms that each require separate explanation
- Hiding uncertainty or debate when the topic genuinely has it
